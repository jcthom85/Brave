# First-Hour Co-op Pass

## Purpose

This document defines what the first hour of `Brave` should feel like for two or more family members playing together.

The game already has the tutorial branch, the cellar, Goblin Road, and the first boss. The co-op pass exists to make that chapter feel fair, readable, and encouraging in actual family play rather than only in solo play.

## Co-op Goals

The first hour should make a family party feel:

- smoother than solo, not slower than solo
- forgiving when one player falls behind or gets knocked out
- clear about where to regroup and what to do next
- rewarding for everyone who took part

## First-Hour Co-op Spine

The intended shared chapter remains:

1. `Wayfarer's Yard`
2. `Rats in the Kettle`
3. `Roadside Howls`
4. `Fencebreakers`
5. `Ruk the Fence-Cutter`

The tutorial must still work solo, but a second player should be able to join without breaking the flow.

## Design Rules

### 1. Party Play Should Help

Two players should feel a little safer and more capable than one player, especially in:

- the cellar
- early Goblin Road pulls
- `Fencebreaker Camp`

The game should not assume perfect play from both players in order for the party to succeed.

### 2. Participation Should Count

If a player meaningfully participated in a fight, the game should avoid punishing them out of the chapter because they were the first one knocked down.

That means the first-hour pass should prefer:

- shared defeat-credit on party encounters
- shared victory credit for party members who took part
- progression that still advances if one player is recovering while the others finish the pull

### 3. Recovery Should Be Gentle

Early defeat should feel like a setback, not a derailment.

The town reset is acceptable, but the player should not feel like:

- they lost the whole quest step
- they missed the family victory entirely
- they now have to grind back into sync alone

### 4. The Road Should Stay Readable

Party movement must remain understandable in the first hour:

- `party follow` should make the road easier, not stranger
- `party where` should be enough to recover from most small separations
- fight starts should pull present party members in cleanly

## Current Implementation Targets

The first co-op pass should focus on:

- slightly friendlier early encounter scaling for duo and trio play
- shared encounter quest credit for registered participants
- shared victory rewards for participating party members, even if they were knocked out before the finish
- clearer party-victory messaging after a group win

## Not In This Pass

This pass does not try to solve everything about multiplayer. It is not yet:

- a shared quest journal system
- a fully synchronized party campaign state
- a late-game raid/boss balance pass

Those are later systems. The goal here is simpler: make the first hour feel good with family.
