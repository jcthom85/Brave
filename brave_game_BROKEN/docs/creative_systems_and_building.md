# Creative Systems And Building: The Designer's Toolkit

In `Brave`, players aren't just adventurers—they are the architects of the multiverse. This document outlines the tools that allow the family to shape the story, build their own worlds, and take on the role of a "Dungeon Master" (DM).

## 1. Immersive Storytelling (Reading & Choice)
Because `Brave` is a text-based game, the story is the star. These features enhance the "Tabletop RPG" feel:
- **Lore Highlights:** Key names, locations, or artifacts are highlighted in the text. Players can `/look <word>` to see a "Lore Card" with a description, history, or image.
- **Story Prompts:** At major narrative turning points, the game pauses for the party to make a choice. These choices are logged in the **"Family Chronicle"** in Brambleford.
- **Narrator Mode:** A dedicated command to "Read the Room" which can trigger a parent-recorded voiceover or simply present the text in a cinematic way for the family to read together.

## 2. Unlocking Creative Powers (World Building)
As players progress, they earn the right to build their own "Pocket Dimensions" in the Nexus. This is framed as a high-level gameplay reward.
- **The Sandbox Portals:** Private zones where players have `/build` permissions.
- **Building Quests:** Fun tasks that guide the player on how to use the build tools (e.g., "Create a room with a 'Spooky' description to unlock the Ghost NPC").
- **Asset Library:** A collection of pre-made "Blueprints" for rooms, NPCs, and items that players can customize.

## 3. The Storyteller's Command Set
Tools for the family to "DM" for each other during a session:
- **`/event`:** Trigger environmental effects (e.g., `/event earthquake` shakes the text and plays a rumbling sound).
- **`/spawn`:** Drop a surprise item or a "Friendly Mystery NPC" into the room.
- **`/shout`:** Send a message as a specific NPC to guide or tease the other players.

## 4. The Family Chronicle
A shared log located in the Lantern Rest Inn that tracks the "Legend" of the family.
- **Big Wins:** "The Day the Boys Defeated the Pot-King."
- **World Records:** "Biggest Fish Caught," "Most Creative Room Built."
- **Story Branches:** A summary of the choices the family has made and how they changed the world.

## 5. Phase-1 Creative Priorities
1. **The Lore Card System:** A basic `/lore` command for 50 key world terms.
2. **The First Sandbox:** A blank "White Room" in the Nexus where the kids can try their first `/build` commands.
3. **The Family Chronicle:** A basic text-based board in the Inn that records the names of bosses defeated.
4. **Basic Event Triggers:** Three simple `/event` commands (Rumble, Flash, Sparkle).
