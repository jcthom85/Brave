# Brave: Writing & Tone Style Guide

This document defines the prose style, character voices, and environmental descriptions for **Brave**. Use this as a reference for manual writing and as a prompt-base for AI-generated content.

## 1. The Core Tone: "Grounded Vigilance"

Brambleford isn't a high-fantasy kingdom of shining towers; it's a frontier town made of timber, slate, and lantern-light. 

*   **Warmth vs. Shadow:** The game exists in the tension between the safety of the hearth and the growing reach of the "long shadows." 
*   **Tactile Reality:** Focus on physical, sensory details: the smell of wet wool, the grit of clawed mud, the splintering of a fence rail, the weight of a wayfarer’s clasp.
*   **Earned Hope:** It is not grimdark. There is kindness and community, but it is a community that keeps its blades sharp and its lanterns lit.

## 2. Prose Pillars

*   **Active & Immediate:** Use active verbs. Avoid passive voice and unnecessary adverbs.
    *   *Bad:* "The door was slowly opened by the wind."
    *   *Good:* "The wind kicks the door wide."
*   **Sensory First:** Every description should lead with a non-visual sense (sound, smell, or temperature) to ground the player.
*   **Economy of Language:** Keep it punchy. We are building for a web/mobile interface; the player should never be "drowning" in text. 3-4 sentences for a room description is the sweet spot.

## 3. Character Profiles (The NPC "Voice")

### Sergeant Tamsin Vale (The Stern Gatekeeper)
*   **Vibe:** Efficient, no-nonsense, protective. She carries "alarm-bell tension" in her shoulders.
*   **Speech Style:** Short, declarative sentences. Uses military terminology (orders, steady, useful).
*   **Key Phrase:** "We get you steady, then we get you useful."

### Captain Harl Rowan (The Immovable Mentor)
*   **Vibe:** Stoic, weary, fatherly but distant. He has seen too many panicked recruits.
*   **Speech Style:** Deep, resonant, patient. He speaks in terms of survival and "start close." He doesn't sugarcoat the danger.
*   **Key Phrase:** "Town's done teaching you what it can."

### Quartermaster Nella (The Sharp Watcher)
*   **Vibe:** Detail-oriented, pragmatic, perhaps a bit cynical about gear maintenance.
*   **Speech Style:** Rapid, listing items, focusing on functionality. She cares about straps, buckles, and weight.
*   **Key Phrase:** "The road has an opinion on your straps."

### Uncle Pib Underbough (The Civilian Heart)
*   **Vibe:** Warm, frazzled, hospitable but stressed by the encroaching shadows.
*   **Speech Style:** More colloquial and emotive. Uses domestic metaphors (bread, hearth, flour).
*   **Key Phrase:** "The cellar is tearing itself apart."

## 4. Anatomy of a Room Description

To keep the world consistent, follow this 3-part structure for every room:

1.  **The Atmosphere:** Lead with a sensory detail (The smell of rain, the distant ring of a bell).
2.  **The Landmark:** Describe the most important physical feature of the room (The stacks of crates, the heavy oak door).
3.  **The Hook:** A final line that hints at the current state of the world or local tension (The newest ink is still dark on the board).

**Example:**
> The smell of cedar and old paper hangs heavy here. A massive slate board dominates the wall, its surface covered in chalk-white names and scribbled dates. One fresh entry at the bottom stands out, written in a hand that was clearly in a hurry.

## 5. Banned "Fantasy Tropes"

*   **Avoid:** "Ye Olde" speak. No *thee, thou, or huzzah*. 
*   **Avoid:** Generic "Magical" descriptions. Don't just say something is "glowing with magic"—describe the *type* of light (a cold blue flicker, a thrumming violet heat).
*   **Avoid:** The "Chosen One" narrative. The player is a hero because they are **Brave** enough to do the work, not because of a prophecy.
