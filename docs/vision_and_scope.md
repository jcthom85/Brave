# Vision And Scope

## Pitch

`Brave` is a family-focused fantasy MUD for 1 to 4 local players, built around a warm town, dangerous roads, tactical party combat, cozy side activities, and portal worlds that let the game grow beyond one genre.

## Player Promise

Players should quickly feel that they are:

- protecting a town worth caring about
- building a distinct hero with readable class identity
- playing together without needing MMO-scale population
- alternating between danger, recovery, town flavor, and discovery
- stepping through portals without losing their character's core identity

## Current Product Shape

The current build supports:

- account and character front-door flow
- seven fantasy classes: Warrior, Cleric, Ranger, Mage, Rogue, Paladin, Druid
- five playable races from the content pack
- Wayfarer's Yard tutorial onboarding
- Brambleford as the central family hub
- a first-hour road chapter ending with Ruk the Fence-Cutter
- a full fantasy ladder through Drowned Weir and The Hollow Lantern
- Junk-Yard Planet as the first portal branch
- ATB combat, quests, loot, gear, forging, mastery, chapel blessings, tinkering, fishing, cooking, shopkeeping, trophies, and local co-op party support
- browser panels, mobile-oriented UI work, and audio hooks

## Design Pillars

### Small But Real

Every live area should feel authored, playable, and finishable. A smaller complete chapter is worth more than a large vague world.

### Family First

The game should work for solo play, a parent and child, or a small local family party. Co-op should make the experience richer without making solo onboarding brittle.

### Town Worth Protecting

Brambleford is the emotional home. Quests, bosses, trophies, NPC reactions, and side activities should all route meaning back to the town.

### Tactical But Readable

Combat can have timing, class roles, reactions, and boss pressure, but the player must always understand what changed and what to do next.

### Portals Without Losing The Core

Portal worlds can change genre, tone, enemies, and ability skins, but the player should still recognize their hero, party, progression, and core verbs.

### Story Through Play

Brave should tell story through place, action, NPC handoffs, readable objects, enemy intros, and town consequences. Long exposition should be rare.

## Near-Term Scope Guardrails

In scope:

- making the first hour excellent
- tightening quest handoffs and tracked objectives
- making Ruk feel like the first chapter payoff
- keeping Brambleford reactive to wins
- strengthening family co-op and recovery loops
- polishing live systems already present

Out of scope until the first hour is excellent:

- adding many new zones
- adding more classes
- adding unrelated portal branches
- building huge creator automation
- writing lore that does not affect play

## Success Criteria

The project is on track when a fresh player can:

- create a character without help
- complete the tutorial and understand the core verbs
- care about Brambleford within minutes
- finish the first road chapter through Ruk
- know what branch to try next
- play alone or with family without the game flow breaking
