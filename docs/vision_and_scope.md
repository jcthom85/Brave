# Vision And Scope

## One-Sentence Pitch

`Brave` is a family-focused fantasy MUD for 1 to 4 local players, serving as a cozy hangout, a creative sandbox for storytelling, and a tactical RPG built around a compact campaign and town-side minigames.

## Player Promise

Players should quickly feel that they are:

- Building a distinct hero in a world that respects their creativity.
- Protecting a town worth caring about, then pushing outward through a readable fantasy campaign.
- Engaging in diverse activities—from intense boss fights to relaxing fishing or running a shop.
- Exploring a rich, reactive audio environment that makes the text come alive.

## Design Pillars

### Small But Real (The Core "Brave" Experience)
The core fantasy game should feel finished and polished, providing a solid foundation before branching into other universes.

### Family Hangout & Shared Storytelling
The game is a safe, warm space for a family to bond. Systems should encourage cooperation, joint narrative choices, and a "Tabletop RPG" vibe.

### Chapter-Based Expansion
The game should grow by finishing one strong fantasy chapter at a time. New regions and side activities should deepen Brambleford's world before any broader thematic expansion is considered.

### Varied Gameplay (Minigames)
Combat isn't the only way to progress. Fishing, cooking, shopkeeping, and resource management are first-class systems with their own rewards and depth.

### Immersive Audio Landscape
High-quality ambient soundscapes, reactive sound effects, and musical themes transform the text-based interface into a multisensory experience.

### Early-MMO Energy
Evoke the structure of classic online RPGs: town hubs, combat zones, named bosses, and loot, but scaled for a small family party.

## Phase-1 Product Shape

Phase 1 establishes the "Home Base" and a complete first fantasy chapter:

- **Brambleford Hub:** The primary safe zone and family hangout.
- **Classic RPG Loop:** Level 1-10 progression in the "Brave" fantasy setting.
- **The Hobbyist's Kit:** Basic fishing and cooking minigames.
- **Audio Foundation:** Ambient loops for all major zones and basic combat SFX.

## Scope Guardrails

These are in scope for phase 1:

- Core combat roles and status effects.
- 2-3 deep minigames (Fishing, Cooking).
- Audio trigger system for the webclient.
- Simple world-building tools for parents/kids.

These are out of scope unless later prioritized:

- Large-scale automated economy.
- Massive open-world generation.
- Competitive PvP.
- Large-scale setting pivots or genre swaps.

## Success Criteria

Phase 1 is successful if it delivers the following:

- A family can play together, alternating between questing and relaxing activities like fishing.
- The audio system successfully enhances the mood without being intrusive.
- The text is readable and engaging for 8-10 year olds (clear descriptions, fun dialogue).
- The **Design & Creation** aspect begins, with players helping define a room or an NPC.
