# Phase-1 Capstone Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the intended end of Brave's current fantasy chapter.

The game now has a real early ladder and a real midgame ladder. What it has needed is a final authored payoff that makes the Blackfen thread land as a chapter ending instead of just another stop on the road. The capstone exists to close phase 1 cleanly and hand the family into whatever comes next with a sense that they finished something meaningful.

## Capstone Summary

- **Zone:** Drowned Weir
- **Quest Spine:** `The South Light` -> `Locks Under Blackwater` -> `The Hollow Lantern`
- **Primary Mentor:** Joss Veller
- **Supporting Voices:** Mira Fenleaf, Mayor Elric Thorne, Captain Harl Rowan, the town notice board
- **Boss:** The Hollow Lantern
- **Shared Trophy:** Hollow Lantern Prism

## Design Goals

The capstone should:

- feel like the natural answer to the wrong lights and bad intent hinted at in Blackfen
- stay compact and deliberate rather than turning into another long wilderness branch
- provide a stronger chapter-ending mood than the zones before it
- work for solo players and family co-op
- give Brambleford a clear reaction to the win
- leave the next major branch open without making phase 1 feel unfinished

## Placement In The Ladder

The capstone comes after:

1. Goblin Road
2. Whispering Woods
3. Old Barrow Field
4. Ruined Watchtower
5. Goblin Warrens
6. Blackfen Approach

It begins after `Miretooth's Claim`.

## Zone Shape

`Drowned Weir` should be a short, high-intent branch beyond Miretooth's Wallow. It should feel like the player has pushed past the ordinary edge of Blackfen and found the source of the wrong southern light.

### Rooms

1. `Drowned Causeway`
2. `Lantern Weir`
3. `Sluice Walk`
4. `Sunken Lock`
5. `Blackwater Lamp House`

### Layout Goals

- short enough to feel like a capstone run, not a second marsh campaign
- one compact map region
- enough structure to feel like an authored place with a past
- one final room that clearly reads as a boss chamber

## Tone

`Drowned Weir` should feel:

- drowned
- cold
- deliberate
- wrongly illuminated
- like an old civic structure that kept doing its job long after the job stopped making sense

This is not raw wilderness. It is old human work gone wrong.

## Enemy Set

The capstone should use a small dedicated enemy family:

- `Drowned Warder`
- `Hollow Wisp`
- `Silt Stalker`
- `The Hollow Lantern`

### Combat Lessons

- wards and add-clearing matter
- support enemies left alone make the fight worse
- movement through a short dangerous branch should feel tense, not grindy
- the boss should reward party focus without requiring a party

## Boss Plan: The Hollow Lantern

The Hollow Lantern should feel like the wrong south light given shape.

### Fantasy Read

- an old lamp-presence or bound marsh-light wearing the drowned lamp house as a crown
- less beastly than Miretooth, more uncanny and intentional
- a clean phase-ending escalation from predator -> place-spirit

### Mechanical Read

- summons support when pressured
- gains a ward while the support holds
- loses that ward when the support dies
- becomes more dangerous at low health
- should use existing combat language already present in Brave rather than needing a new system

## Quest Chain

### 1. The South Light

Purpose:

- tells the player Blackfen was not the whole problem
- sends them from town back into the marsh with a new explanation
- opens the capstone branch with exploration-first goals

### 2. Locks Under Blackwater

Purpose:

- confirms the drowned weir is active
- makes the player push through the middle rooms
- establishes the local enemy family before the boss

### 3. The Hollow Lantern

Purpose:

- delivers the chapter-ending boss
- gives the current Brambleford chapter its cleanest town-scale payoff
- awards the shared trophy and closes the south-light problem

## Payoff Rules

The capstone should end with:

- a clear victory screen
- a quest completion with strong chapter-close language
- Trophy Hall support
- Joss and Mayor reactions that acknowledge the chapter is really over
- the town notice board updating to reflect the win

## Scope Guardrails

Do not let this turn into:

- another long branch like Blackfen
- a new major system
- a second unrelated expansion arc
- a lore dump

The capstone should be short, atmospheric, and decisive.

## Implementation Notes

During implementation:

- add the capstone doc before or alongside content work
- reuse the existing quest and boss pattern structure
- prefer one compact new region over multiple subregions
- keep the boss readable in co-op
- make the town reaction part of the shipped slice, not a later polish item
